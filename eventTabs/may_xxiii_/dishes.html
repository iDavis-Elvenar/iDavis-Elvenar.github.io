..:: Dishes ::..
Dishes are the main target of the event! You need dishes to satisfy customers and complete their offers (you can find a detailed list of all offers in the tab Offers).

There are 3 types of dishes in total and each consists of 6 different levels (tiers). You can review all the available dishes in the table below:

Name of the dish Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
Hot Chocolate
Lime Dessert
Delicious Roast
When you take a closer look at the Stockpiles tab, you can see how the dishes are spawned in your kitchen. Generally speaking, it is more likely that the spawning process itself will give you some of the low-level dishes and you will need to start the merging process to get the following tiers. The merging process is where the core of this mechanic lies!

In order to start the merging process, you need to have 2 dishes of the same type and the same level in your kitchen. By dragging one of these dishes and dropping onto the other, you merge them and create one dish of the following level (and the same type, of course).
By adding low-level dishes to your kitchen and repeating the merging process on them, you can receive the required dishes to fulfill your offers. You can use the interactive visualization below to see how the merging process works (click the Play button below to start the animation and use the respective Merge nodes to merge your generated dishes (if the required low-level dishes are available, of course)).
From the visualization we can easily see that the higher the level of a dish is, the more level 1 dishes you need and the whole process gets much slower at progress. Now, let's realize the costs of high-level dishes.

..:: Average scenario ::..

In this section we will focus on calculating the average costs of different levels. It will help you create a general idea about the costs of customers' offers.

Let's consider we have 400 chef hats at our disposal, which makes it possible to open a respective stockpile 100 items. Sticking with the percentual values (from the Stockpiles tab), we will get the following output:

  • 40x dish level 1
  • 30x dish level 2
  • 15x dish level 3
  • 10x dish level 4
  • 5x dish level 5
  • 0x dish level 6
We have received 40x dish level 1 from the spawning process, which means we clicked the stockpile 40 times. Each time we paid 4 chef hats, so 40 pieces level 1 cost 40*4=160 chef hats in total. Because we earned 40 dishes in total, it makes 160/40=4 chef hats per one level 1 dish.

Let's calculate the level 2, assuming we want to get the maximal number of level 2 dishes from our stack above. We have 40 dishes level 1 for which we paid 160 chef hats. Out of these we can merge 20 dishes level 2. We already have 30 dishes level 2, for which we paid 30*4=120 chef hats, so in total, we have 20+30=50 dishes level 2, which cost us 160+120=280 chef hats. From that we get the cost of 280/50=5.6 chef hats per one level 2 piece.

Let's go with one more example, calculating the level 3. We have 40 dishes level 1, which we can merge into 20 dishes level 2, that can be further merged into 10 dishes level 3 (this cost us 40*4=160 chef hats). We also have 30 dishes level 2, out of which we can merge 15 dishes level 3 (we paid 30*4=120 chef hats for 30 dishes level 2). Finally, from our stack above we also have 15 dishes level 3, for which we paid 15*4=60 chef hats. That makes 160+120+60=340 chef hats and 10+15+15=40 dishes level 3 in total. So, we get the cost of 340/40=8.5 chef hats per one level 3 piece.

Similarly, we can calculate all the remaining levels and get the following table:
Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
4 5.6 8.5 12.7 18.4 38.9
Last but not least, it's important to point out that some customer offers may ask for different combinations of dishes everytime they appear. You can see the list of all offers in the Offers tab and see, what for the cost ranges they have (the costs are always displayed as the number of required level 1 dishes) and so you can find yourself whether your current offer is on the lower side of this range, or if it is more beneficial for you to decline the offer and complete some other offers in the meantime.
..:: Worst-case scenario ::..

Now that we have covered the average scenario, let's focus on the worst possible one. The values we calculate here are used in the Offers tab, so that you can have an idea what are the worst possible prizes for the respective offers (to help you preparing your chef hats). Keep in mind that none of this knowledge is required for progressing in event. It only helps those who are interested in the "behind the scene" calculations and math.

Let's consider, how many level 1 dishes we need for a specific dish of level N.
We know that we need 2 dishes of level L to merge one dish of level L+1. In other words, every following level doubles the required number of level L dishes. Let's consider the following tree for merging the level 4 dish:

How many level 1 dishes do we need for one dish of level N. From the tree visualization above we can see how many times we have doubled the amount - this can be observed from the number of arrows between the dish of level N (which in our case is 4) and the dish we want to find the amount of, which is 1:

Such a distance is 3, simply because the difference between 4 and 1 is 3. That makes it possible to realize how the amount of level 1 dishes to merge level N can be calculated:

2^(N-1)
Which makes it possible to realize the following table and calculate, how many times would we need to open stockpiles in the worst-case scenario:
Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
1 2 4 8 16 32
Which in the worst-case scenario would cost *4 event currency. Let's take the following offer as an example:


The offer requires Hot Chocolate level 5 and Delicious Roast level 3. The Hot Chocolate level 5 requires 2^(5-1) hot chocolates level 1 (which is 16) and the Delicious Roast level 3 requires 2^(3-1) delicious roasts level 1 (which is 4). In total, we need 20 dishes of level 1 to fulfill this offer, which, in the worst case scenario, would cost 20*4=80 of event currency (chef hats). Because the Stockpiles can also generate dishes of higher level than 1, we have a good chance that the final cost will be actually lower (see the Cups tab to see how exactly the dishes are generated). This is where the nice, randomized mechanic comes into play.
