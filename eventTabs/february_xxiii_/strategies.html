..:: Strategies ::..
The Great Excavation is my favorite event type as it is the only mechanic that allows players to fully pull out their strategic skills. Unlike rotating chests or shuffle board events, the tile event gives you much better control over the rewards and progress you receive (which in my opinion makes the event much more interesting after all).

In the Tools tab we have described all the tools you have at your disposal. Each of the tools has its own special effect and it's up to you to use them as efficiently as possible! I'm going to describe several well proven strategies that may help you on your way to maximizing your rewards.


  • Best ways of using Shovel. Shovel reveals one single tile you click on and it is the only tool that can be used on a dusty tile. Out of all available tools, it's the most beneficial one towards moving forward in the city. It can also come handy when you want to pick up rewards you left behind (and if they are placed far from each other) or to prepare your space for using other tools.
  • Best ways of using Bottled Wind. Bottled Wind is extremely useful for receiving information about the surrounding area. It can only be used on an already cleared tile and it reveals a column of dusty tiles (the wind won't blow behind the obstacle, so make sure to wisely choose the column in which you maximize the affected area). You can also maximize the information you receive if you make sure that the surrounding columns have as many unrevealed tiles as possible.
  • Best ways of using Vacuum Vial. Similarly to Bottled Wind, it can only be used on an empty tile. It is the best idea to use Vacuum Vial when many rewards are placed close to each other, but spread in different columns. It may also come handy if you just want to move forward in the city but many obstacles are laid out in your way.
  • Do not clear every tile! There are many rewards hidden inside the Buried City, but not all of them contain prizes. Learn about different states of the tiles: empty tile (when no more dust needs to be removed and you can immediately claim the reward), semi-visible tile (you can see whether it contains rewards or not but you need to clear the dust first before you can claim it) and the fully dusted tiles (you cannot see whether it contains prizes or not and you need to clear some of the adjacent tiles first to find out). You can take advantage of what you know about different states of visibility and use these tips to maximize your rewards. If you see that there is no reward hidden the semi-visible tile, there is no reason to clear it. Using your tools strategically will lead to maximizing your information about the surrounding tiles and minimizing the number of fully dusted tiles you should leave behind!
  • The Golden Eye! This is the newest addition of the Great Excavation event! For each revealed tile which does not contain a reward your receive Power. Power is the resource to charge the Golden Eye and once it's fully charged, you can use its divine effect to reveal locations of all rewards within the currently visible area (but be careful, once it's fully charged, any additional Power collected will be lost). In other words, if you do not manage to get the most out of the tools (using steps I have described above), any extra effect will be taken in favor of Golden Eye!
