..:: Pieces ::..
Pieces are the main target of the event! You need pieces to trade with dwarves and complete their offers (you can find a detailed list of these offers in the tab Trades).

There are 3 types of pieces in total and each consists of 6 different levels (tiers). You can review all available items in the table below:

Name of the piece Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
Dwarven Figurines
Dwarven Tools
Dices
When you take a closer look at the Cups tab, you can see more closely how the items are spawned on your board. Generally speaking, it is more likely that you receive some of the low-level items from the spawning process and you need to start the merging process to get the following tiers. The merging process is where the core of this mechanic lies!

In order to start the merging process, you need to have 2 items of the same type and the same level on your board. By dragging one of these items and dropping onto the other, you merge these items and out of this process one item of the following level (and the same type, of course) is created!

By adding the low-level items to your board and repeating the merging process on these items, you can receive the required pieces for fulfilling your tasks. You can use the interactive visualization below to see how the merging process works (click the Play button below to start the animation and use the respective Merge nodes to merge your generated items (if the required low-level items are available, of course)).
From the visualization we can easily see that the higher the item is, the more level 1 items you need and the whole process gets much slower. Now, let's realize, how many level 1 items we need for a specific item of level N.
We know that we need 2 items of level L to merge one item of level L+1. In other words, every following level doubles the required number of level L items. Let's consider the following tree for merging the level 4 item:

How many level 1 items we need for one item of level N. From the tree visualization above we can see how many times we have doubled the amount - this can be read from the number of arrows between the item of level N (which in our case is 4) and the item we want to find the amount of, which is 1:

Such a distance is 3, simply because the difference between 4 and 1 is 3. That makes it possible to realize how the amount of level 1 items to merge level N can be calculated:

2^(N-1)
Which makes it possible to realize the following table and calculate, how many times would we need to open cups in the worst-case scenario:
Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
1 2 4 8 16 32
Which in the worst-case scenario would cost *5 event currency. Let's take the following offer as an example:


The offer requires Tool level 3 and Dice level 6. The Tool level 3 requires 2^(3-1) tools level 1 (which is 4) and the Dice level 6 requires 2^(6-1) dices level 1 (which is 32). In total, we need 36 items level 1 to fulfill this offer, which, in the worst case scenario, would cost 36*5=180 of event currency (dwarven chips). Because the Cups can also generate the higher-level items than 1, we have a good chance that the final cost will be lower (see the Cups tab to see how exactly these items are generated). This is where the nice, randomized mechanic comes into play.

After all, it's important to point out that some offers may have different costs everytime they appear. You can see the list of all offers in the Trades tab and see, what for the cost ranges they have (the costs' values are always displayed as the number of level 1 items needed) and so you can find yourself whether your current offer is on the lower end of this range, or if it is more beneficial for you to decline the offer and complete some other tasks in the meantime.
