..:: Pieces ::..
Pieces are the main target of the event! You need pieces to trade with dwarves and complete their offers (you can find a detailed list of these offers in the tab Trades).

There are 3 types of pieces in total and each consists of 6 different levels (tiers). You can review all available items in the table below:

Name of the piece Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
Dwarven Figurines
Dwarven Tools
Dices
When you take a closer look at the Cups tab, you can see more closely how the pieces are spawned on your board. Generally speaking, it is more likely that you receive some of the low-level pieces from the spawning process and you need to start the merging process to get the following tiers. The merging process is where the core of this mechanic lies!

In order to start the merging process, you need to have 2 pieces of the same type and the same level on your board. By dragging one of these pieces and dropping onto the other, you merge these pieces and out of this process one piece of the following level (and the same type, of course) is created!

By adding the low-level pieces to your board and repeating the merging process on these pieces, you can receive the required pieces to fulfill your tasks. You can use the interactive visualization below to see how the merging process works (click the Play button below to start the animation and use the respective Merge nodes to merge your generated pieces (if the required low-level pieces are available, of course)).
From the visualization we can easily see that the higher the piece is, the more level 1 pieces you need and the whole process gets much slower. Let's realize the costs of high-level pieces.

..:: Worst-case scenario ::..

Let's consider, how many level 1 pieces we need for a specific piece of level N.
We know that we need 2 pieces of level L to merge one piece of level L+1. In other words, every following level doubles the required number of level L pieces. Let's consider the following tree for merging the level 4 piece:

How many level 1 pieces we need for one piece of level N. From the tree visualization above we can see how many times we have doubled the amount - this can be read from the number of arrows between the piece of level N (which in our case is 4) and the piece we want to find the amount of, which is 1:

Such a distance is 3, simply because the difference between 4 and 1 is 3. That makes it possible to realize how the amount of level 1 pieces to merge level N can be calculated:

2^(N-1)
Which makes it possible to realize the following table and calculate, how many times would we need to open cups in the worst-case scenario:
Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
1 2 4 8 16 32
Which in the worst-case scenario would cost *5 event currency. Let's take the following offer as an example:


The offer requires Tool level 3 and Dice level 6. The Tool level 3 requires 2^(3-1) tools level 1 (which is 4) and the Dice level 6 requires 2^(6-1) dices level 1 (which is 32). In total, we need 36 pieces of level 1 to fulfill this offer, which, in the worst case scenario, would cost 36*5=180 of event currency (dwarven chips). Because the Cups can also generate the higher-level pieces than 1, we have a good chance that the final cost will be lower (see the Cups tab to see how exactly these pieces are generated). This is where the nice, randomized mechanic comes into play.

..:: Average scenario ::..

Now, it makes sense to realize a bit more realistic numbers and consider that we most likely won't need to put level 6 together from lots of level 1 pieces only. Let's get away from the worst-case scenario and let's calculate the average costs of specific levels.

Let's consider we have 500 dwarven chips at our disposal, which makes it possible to open a cup 100 items. Sticking with the percentual values (from the Cups tab), we get the following:

  • 38x piece level 1
  • 30x piece level 2
  • 15x piece level 3
  • 10x piece level 4
  • 5x piece level 5
  • 2x piece level 6
We have received 38x piece level 1 from the spawning process only, which means we clicked the cup to get this number 38 times. Each time we paid 5 dwarven chips, so 38 pieces level 1 cost 38*5=190 dwarven chips in total. Because we earned 38 pieces in total, it makes 190/38=5 dwarven chips per one level 1 piece.

Let's calculate the level 2, assuming we want to get the maximal number of level 2 pieces from our stack above. We have 38 pieces level 1 for which we paid 190 chips. Out of these we can merge 19 pieces level 2. We already have 30 pieces level 2, for which we paid 30*5=150 dwarven chips, so in total, we have 19+30=49 pieces level 2, which cost us 190+150=340 dwarven chips. From that we get 340/49=6.9 dwarven chips per one level 2 piece.

Let's go with one more, calculating the level 3. We have 38 pieces level 1, which we can merge into 19 pieces level 2, that can be further merged into 9 pieces level 3 (now realize that we only needed 36 pieces level 1, so this cost us 36*5=180 dwarven chips). We also have 30 pieces level 2, out of which we can merge 15 pieces level 3 (we paid 30*5=150 dwarven chips for 30 pieces level 2). Finally, we have 15 pieces level 3, for which we paid 15*5=75 dwarven chips. That makes 180+150+75=405 dwarven chips and 9+15+15=39 pieces level 3 in total. So, we get 405/39=10.4 dwarven chips per one level 3 piece.

Similarly, we can calculate all the remaining levels and get the following table:
Level 1 Level 2 Level 3 Level 4 Level 5 Level 6

Last but not least, it's important to point out that some trade rewards may ask for different combinations of pieces everytime they appear. You can see the list of all offers in the Trades tab and see, what for the cost ranges they have (the costs' values are always displayed as the number of level 1 pieces needed) and so you can find yourself whether your current offer is on the lower end of this range, or if it is more beneficial for you to decline the offer and complete some other tasks in the meantime.
