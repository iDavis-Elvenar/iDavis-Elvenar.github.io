..:: Traders of Unur ::..
..:: Wonders ::..
The Chapter 17 introduces two new wonders: Dragon Ark and Oracle of Fortune. In this overview, we will take a look at their effects, avatars and I will share with you a couple of tips and tricks on how to get the most out of these wonders and some of my opinions about their future in-game prognosis.

..:: Dragon Ark ::..
Size
 5x4
Effects
 Rewards an amount of Boosted Precious Sentient Goods when accomplishing a quest of the main story
from 18x the number of your completed provinces at level 1 to 138x at level 35
Rewards Combining Catalysts every time you receive a Mystical Object from Crafting
from 2 combining catalysts at level 1 to 14 combining catalysts at level 35
Tips & Evaluation
 In the past, we had the opportunity to collect multiple Moonstone Library Sets from the Spire of Eternity, and many of us took advantage of this chance, allowing us to easily accumulate Combining Catalysts. However, this option is no longer available, making it quite challenging to gather Combining Catalysts now. Despite these changes, I still find the most practical approach to obtaining Combining Catalysts by producing them within the Magic Academy. Given my above-average results in Tournaments, I have an abundant supply of other spells from there, so my Magic Academy is dedicated entirely to producing Combining Catalysts. I haven't faced any issues with their availability for quite some time. While I believe this wonder may offer relief to some players, I personally decided against investing my city's space and resources in it.
The second effect of the wonder is not a motivating factor for me to build it either. There are already numerous other sources for acquiring Sentient Goods, the most significant of which, for me, is the Pyramid of Purification wonder from Chapter 13. With ample access to Sentient Goods from other sources, I do not see the need to prioritize this wonder for its second effect either.

..:: Oracle of Fortune ::..
Size
 5x5
Effects
 Rewards an amount of random Boosted Sentient Goods every time you craft something
from 3x the number of your completed provinces at level 1 to 23x at level 34
Produces Royal Restoration weekly
from 1 royal restoration spell at level 1 to 7 royal restoration spells at level 31
Tips & Evaluation
 Similar to the Dragon Ark wonder, I find little reason to build an additional wonder solely for the production of Sentient Goods.
The usefulness of the second effect heavily relies on the performance of your fellowship in Tournaments. Tournaments serve as the primary source for Royal Restoration spells, and if your fellowship can reach all the chests, you can receive up to 14 Royal Restoration spells weekly. This is twice the number provided by this wonder at its highest levels, leading me to question whether it is truly worthwhile to invest resources and city space in constructing it. The final decision should depend on your city's specific needs, particularly considering how much you rely on event buildings, how frequently you replace them with new ones, or how often you choose to upgrade them to higher chapters instead. Weighing these factors will help you determine whether the wonder aligns with your city's priorities and objectives.
Also note that while I do recommend having a few event culture-population hybrids in your city, I advise against relying solely on them. There are wonders and game mechanics that allow you to benefit the most from Residential buildings. For instance, consider the Thermal Spring of Youth wonder in Chapter 15, which increases the population in each Residential building. Additionally, the Community Work introduced in Chapter 21 is exclusively provided by Residential buildings.
So having an excessive number of event buildings can not only lead to negative consequences in terms of the intended Game Design, but may also result in exceeding the requirements for Royal Restoration spells.
Rating
 5/10
