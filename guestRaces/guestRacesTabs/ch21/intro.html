..:: The Vallorian Legend ::..
..:: Intro ::..

Captain Bonkers, leader of the Pirate Guild, pays us a visit and brags that he found a yet unknown island of giant proportions, filled with precious treasures. He offers some mysterious pearls he found there, that are said to bring prosperity to whoever possesses them. While our Mentor is a bit skeptical of buying stuff whose origin is unknown, our Trader is very fascinated and convinces everyone to accept the offer.

Just when Captain Bonkers has left, a horde of dragon-riding Orcs attacks the town, including an angry Orc warlady. It turns out that Captain Bonkers stole the pearls from the warlady of the Orc tribe that lives there - and they followed his traces all the way to your town.
..:: What's new in this chapter? ::..
Prosperity
Prosperity is a new requirement next to Culture and Population. It is provided by the core city buildings, such as Main Hall, Barracks, Residences and Workshops. Prosperity will be required from Chapter 21 onwards to construct and upgrade Settlement buildings.

After unlocking the very first technology of this chapter, the user interface will get updated so that you can see Prosperity right next to your Culture and Population requirements:


Click on the drop-down button to select the default displayed requirement and hover over the four-leaf clover to see your current state of all requirements in your city.


Note that only buildings upgraded to levels unlocked by Chapter 21 start to provide Prosperity. Make sure you have upgraded some of your core city buildings before you get into building the Vallorian Legend's settlement.

Community Work
Community Work is a new Discovered Resource (similarly to Orcs, Mana, Seeds and Unurium). It is provided by Residential Buildings, which now offer a switchable production: you can decide, whether your citizens pay taxes or provide Community Work. This resource will be required in many places of the game from Chapter 21 onwards!
