..:: Team Spirit ::..
..:: Wonders ::..
The Chapter 18 introduces two new wonders: Shrine of the Champions and Temple of Spirits. In this overview, we will take a look at their effects, avatars and I will share with you a couple of tips and tricks on how to get the most out of these wonders and some of my opinions about their future in-game prognosis.

..:: Shrine of the Champions ::..
Size
 3x4
Effects
 Produces an amount of Gruff Orc Warrior every 3 hours (as a percentage of your current Units per Squad)
from 22.5% of your squad size at level 1 to 172.5% of your squad size at level 35
Rewards an amount of Orcs per Tournament Point (depending on the Tournament points you achieved and your required culture)
from 0.00024% of your required culture per one tournament point at level 1 to 0.00184% at level 35
Tips & Evaluation
 If, for instance, you have 1,000,000 required culture, the fully upgraded wonder would yield 92,000 orcs weekly if you achieve 5,000 points in the Tournament. While this number may seem appealing, I have discovered more efficient strategies to produce orcs in my city, rendering the construction of an additional wonder unnecessary. I focus on incorporating more Armories into my city. They enhance the effect of Military buildings, particularly when combined with Brown Bears, and consume a significant amount of culture (while remaining relatively inexpensive in terms of population). This not only aids in ranking but also boosts the output of several other wonders that rely on required culture. For example, my city's significant number of armories greatly elevates the production of the second wonder in this chapter, the Temple of Spirits, which eliminates the need for additional Unurium production in the Trader and also eliminates the need for constructing even bigger (and less efficient) wonder, the Vallorial Seal Tower in Chapter 19 (with the same focus on Unurium).
So in short, I prefer alternative possibilities to gather this resource more effectively rather than constructing an additional wonder that would have no impact on other productions and effects within my city, despite its small size. I have decided to not build this wonder in my city.
I do not consider the secondary effect to be a compelling reason for constructing this wonder either.

..:: Temple of Spirits ::..
Size
 7x4
Effects
 Rewards an amount of Unurium per Spire Chest (depending on your Required Culture)
from 0.15% of your required culture at level 1 to 1.15% at level 35
Rewards an amount of Unurium per Event Currency Pickup from around your city (depending on your Required Culture)
from 0.075% of your required culture at level 1 to 0.575% at level
Tips & Evaluation
 To fully capitalize on the benefits of this wonder, your primary focus should be on maximizing the amount of required culture in your city, as both of its effects heavily depend on it. I have personally found success through a strategic approach involving Armories. By significantly increasing the number of Armories in my city, I managed to consume a significant amount of culture while requiring a relatively small population, enabling me to construct numerous Armories without significant strain.
For instance, let's assume you have 1,000,000 required culture, which is approximately the average number for a Chapter 18 player. With this city, the wonder will yield a remarkable 552,000 Unurium weekly from the Spire of Eternity (a completion I highly recommend). Moreover, you may also receive an additional 86,250 Unurium daily if there is an ongoing event and you diligently collect event currency in the city outskirts.
This approach not only grants you significantly more Unurium weekly compared to the second Unurium-focused wonder in the following chapter, the Vallorian Seal Tower, but it also allows you to entirely cease Unurium production in the Trader. Thus, there is no longer any need for switching productions between Unurium and Seeds, providing a more efficient resource management system in your city.
The wonder is not the smallest one; however, I highly recommend it to anyone facing Unurium deficiency as it may offer a definitive solution.
